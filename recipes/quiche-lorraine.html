Quiche Lorraine

Description

Chef John's Quiche Lorraine, as seen on Allrecipes.com

This is a rich, fluffy, and custardy Quiche Lorraine that's about equal parts egg, cream, and milk. The bacon, leeks, onion, and Gruyère cheese provide enough flavor to keep spices to a minimum.

Use thick-cut bacon, cook fully, and avoid strong flavors like maple. I prefer black pepper crusted (cut the pepper in the leeks and onion), plain, or hickory/applewood/cherrywood in that order.

Ingredients

  • Pie crust (9" deep dish) - pappy's if not homemade
  • 8 slices bacon (thick-cut) cut to 1" pieces
  • 1/2 cup chopped leeks
  • 1/2 cup chopped yellow onion
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to tast
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper, more to taste
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 yolks
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh thyme
  • 6 oz shredded Gruyère cheese

Steps

  1. Prep pie dough, press into pie plate, chill at least 20 minutes
  2. Preaheat oven for your pie dough pre-bake. Follow instructions of your preferred recipe.
  3. Blind bake chilled pie crust following preferred recipe, fill with baking weights/rice/beans/upside down method.
  4. While preheating/baking crust, fully cook bacon in skillet over medium heat, 10 minutes or so. Chop leeks and onions.
  5. Remove bacon and all but 1-2 tsp of oil. Add leaks, onion, salt, pepper, cayenne. Cook and stir until golden brown, 5-7 minutes.
  6. Remove crust from oven if you havent yet, reduce temp to 325 °F
  7. Whisk eggs, yolks, cream, and milk. add thyme and stir to combine.
  8. Add 2/3 of leek and onion mix to the pie crust, then 1/3 of cooked bacon, and 2/3 of Gruyère. Carefully ladle in egg mix making sure not to disturbe the layers. Sprinkle with remaining leeks, bacon, and Gruyère in that order.
    9. leeks, bacon, and Gruyère in that order.
  9. Bake in 325 °F oven until the top is browning, Shifting the quiche around, the center should not be jiggly.
  10. Allow to cool slightly and serve.
